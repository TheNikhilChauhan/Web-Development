1. A King Reborn

In "The Beginning After the End," the story begins with King Grey, a powerful ruler in his previous life, who is reincarnated into a new world as a baby named Arthur Leywin. This new world is filled with magic and fantastical creatures, and Arthur must navigate his new life while holding onto the memories and knowledge from his past life. As he grows, he learns to adapt to his new surroundings, discovering his exceptional talents and magical abilities.

2. The Burden of Power

As Arthur matures, he faces the complexities of his powers and the responsibilities they bring. Despite his strength, he must also contend with the harsh realities of his new life, such as the intricacies of family, politics, and potential enemies. The novel delves into themes of identity, morality, and the consequences of power, as Arthur strives to balance his desires for peace and justice with the challenges of his past and present circumstances.
